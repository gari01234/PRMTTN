Chromatic Patterns for Pre‑verbal Thought (v\u202f2025‑07‑28)

1 · Chromatic Containment

Operational definition. Configure the palette so that every glyph appears as a self‑contained entity, visually delimited by a narrow tonal band (\u2264\u202f30\u202f\u00b0). No outlines, no hard contrast: cohesion is achieved through shared hue, mid–high saturation (60–80\u202f%) and stable value (0.72\u202f\u00b1\u202f0.04). The result is a silent, steady field that fosters focused pre‑verbal attention.

  • Glyphs\u00a0· hues inside \u2264\u202f30\u202f\u00b0, S\u202f60–80\u202f%, V\u202f\u2248\u202f0.72.
  • Background\u00a0· hue\u00a0+\u202f180\u202f\u00b0, S\u202f\u2248\u202f18\u202f%, V\u202f\u2248\u202f0.42.
  • Cube\u00a0· same\u202fhue, S\u202f\u2264\u202f6\u202f%, V\u202f0.26.
  • Contrast\u00a0· \u0394E\u202f24–30.

Expected outcome. Glyphs are clearly separated from the ambience yet cohere with one another, keeping the viewer in a calm, non‑narrative focus.

Scientific ground. Narrow chromatic bands reinforce object completion (Gestalt “good form”). Iso‑chromatic fields show reduced pre‑frontal semantic load while occipital contour analysis stays active (Palmer\u202f&\u202fSchloss 2010).

Refs.\u00a0Koffka\u202f1935; Wertheimer\u202f1923; Palmer & Schloss 2010.

2 · Contrast\u00a0&\u00a0Dissonance

Operational definition. Deliberately collide hues separated by \u2265\u202f120\u202f\u00b0, alternate high saturation and value offsets. No harmony is allowed; the palette keeps the visual system in alert, avoiding semantic closure.

  • Glyphs\u00a0· \u2265\u202f120\u202f\u00b0 jumps, S\u202f65–85\u202f%, V\u202f0.78\u202f\u00b1\u202f0.06.
  • Background\u00a0· hue of the darkest glyph +\u202f40\u202f\u00b0, S\u202f25\u202f%, V\u202f0.35.
  • Cube\u00a0· same\u202fhue, S\u202f\u2264\u202f10\u202f%, V\u202f0.22.
  • Contrast\u00a0· \u0394E\u202f\u2265\u202f35.

Expected outcome. A restless scene: colours vibrate without synthesis, keeping perception raw and pre‑verbal.

Scientific ground. Cognitive disfluency prolongs attention and deepens processing (Alter\u202f&\u202fOppenheimer 2009). Extreme \u0394H elevates N2/P3 components linked to non‑semantic vigilance (Itti\u202f&\u202fKoch 2001).

Refs.\u00a0Alter\u202f&\u202fOppenheimer 2009; Itti\u202f&\u202fKoch 2001; Ramachandran\u202f&\u202fHirstein 1999.

3 · Non‑semantic Disposition

Operational definition. Build palettes that differentiate glyphs without forming culturally coded series (no classic complements, triads, warm/cool scales). Colour remains raw material, extending the pre‑attentive phase where thought precedes language.

  • Glyphs\u00a0· irregular 50–70\u202f\u00b0 spacing, S\u202f40–60\u202f%, V\u202f\u2248\u202f0.70.
  • Background\u00a0· circular mean\u00a0\u00b1\u202f25\u202f\u00b0, S\u202f25\u202f%, V\u202f0.40.
  • Cube\u00a0· same\u202fhue, S\u202f5\u202f%, V\u202f0.25.
  • Contrast\u00a0· \u0394E\u202f22–28.

Scientific ground. Avoiding learned colour associations suspends affective valuation (Palmer\u202f&\u202fSchloss 2010) and dampens pre‑frontal categorisation, favouring open visual exploration (Lafer‑Sousa et al. 2016).

Refs.\u00a0Palmer\u202f&\u202fSchloss 2010; Gibson 1979; Lafer‑Sousa et al. 2016.

4 · Structured Ambiguity

Suggest order by partial gradients (arc\u202f\u2264\u202f90\u202f\u00b0) but break regularity with uneven steps. The eye senses a rule it cannot confirm, sustaining exploration without linguistic anchoring.

  • Glyphs\u00a0· arc\u202f\u2264\u202f90\u202f\u00b0, irregular 12–18\u202f\u00b0, S\u202f45–65\u202f%, V\u202f\u2248\u202f0.65.
  • Background\u00a0· arc-mid\u202f+\u202f110\u202f\u00b0, S\u202f15\u202f%, V\u202f0.50.
  • Cube\u00a0· same\u202fhue, S\u202f6\u202f%, V\u202f0.25.
  • Contrast\u00a0· \u0394E\u202f20–28.

Ground. Moderate ambiguity maximises “processing pleasure” (Reber et al. 2004) and drives low‑level prediction‑error cycles (Muth\u202f&\u202fCarbon 2013), ideal for preverbal tension.

Refs.\u00a0Reber 2004; Muth\u202f&\u202fCarbon 2013; Silvia 2005.

5 · Chromatic Isotropy

Create a focus‑free field by covering the hue circle with equidistant 25–35\u202f\u00b0 steps, constant saturation and value. No colour outweighs the rest; attention spreads laterally.

  • Glyphs\u00a0· steps\u202f25–35\u202f\u00b0, S\u202f55\u202f\u00b1\u202f3\u202f%, V\u202f0.74\u202f\u00b1\u202f0.03.
  • Background\u00a0& Cube\u00a0· neutral grey V\u202f0.60 / 0.55.
  • Contrast\u00a0· \u0394E\u202f26–30.

Ground. Removing salience peaks activates global precedence networks, reducing predictive load and enabling diffuse awareness (Buschman\u202f&\u202fMiller 2007).

Refs.\u00a0Buschman\u202f&\u202fMiller 2007; Vogel\u202f&\u202fMachizawa 2004.

6 · Self‑Sufficient Presence

Assign highly differentiated hues (\u2265\u202f80\u202f\u00b0) but with mid‑low saturation so they do not vie for dominance. Each glyph stands without needing the others.

  • Glyphs\u00a0· \u0394H\u202f\u2265\u202f80\u202f\u00b0, S\u202f35–50\u202f%, V\u202f0.68–0.80.

  • Cube\u00a0· same\u202fhue, V\u202f0.45.
  • Contrast\u00a0· \u0394E\u202f28–34.

Ground. Moderate chromatic distance supports individuation in temporal cortex without symbolic categorisation (Xu\u202f&\u202fChun 2009).

Refs.\u00a0Xu\u202f&\u202fChun 2009; Bays\u202f&\u202fHusain 2008.

7 · Associative Asymmetry

Form loose clusters (\u2264\u202f18\u202f\u00b0 internal) separated by \u2265\u202f55\u202f\u00b0. Associations emerge without symmetry, letting attention wander unpredictably.

  • Glyphs\u00a0· clusters of 2–4, internal \u2264\u202f18\u202f\u00b0, external \u2265\u202f55\u202f\u00b0.

  • Cube\u00a0· background darkened V\u202f\u2212\u202f0.12.

Refs.\u00a0Wagemans 2012; Palmer 1999; Arnheim 1974.

9 · Habitable without Translation

Create a chromatic “climate” (band\u202f60\u202f\u00b0) with low saturation that sustains orientation without evoking cultural codes.

Refs.\u00a0K\u00fcller 2009; Gallagher 2005.

11 · Active Transparency

Select saturated, high‑value hues separated \u2265\u202f130\u202f\u00b0 to maintain chroma after additive blending (\u03b1\u202f\u2248\u202f0.25). Glyphs can overlap without collapsing into grey.

Refs.\u00a0Metelli 1974; de\u202fWeert\u202f&\u202fMausfeld 2003.
