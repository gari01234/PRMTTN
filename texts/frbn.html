FRBN — Deterministic Ganzfeld

What you see cannot be otherwise given the current scene. FRBN does not invent a palette: it extracts it deterministically from the active permutations and the chromatic engine.

  • Source palette. Colors are collected from the glyphs currently on stage (their materials are computed by the same deterministic HSV lattice used in BUILD). If needed, hues are normalised to ensure separation.
  • Pattern coupling. The chromatic pattern (1–11) and the global seed (sceneSeed) bias hue selection in BUILD; FRBN reads the result, not a new random set.
  • Temporal blend. The shader interpolates the collected colors over time. The rate is tied to the average rotation speed of the permutations, so color breathing follows the scene dynamics.
  • No alternatives. For the same set of permutations, mapping and pattern, the FRBN field is reproducible. Change the scene → the field changes; keep the scene → the field must be identical.
  • Banding control. A high‑precision dither (blue‑noise) is applied in linear space to avoid contour banding without altering the palette.

FRBN is therefore a deterministic projection of the current combinatorial state, not a decorative background.
